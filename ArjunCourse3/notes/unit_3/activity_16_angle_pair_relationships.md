## Quick Review Notes

### Main Idea
Angles that share a vertex or a line can be related by their measures. Parallel lines cut by a transversal create predictable equal-angle pairs you can use to find missing measures.

### Key Vocabulary
- **Complementary angles:** Two angles whose measures add to 90¬į.
- **Supplementary angles:** Two angles whose measures add to 180¬į.
- **Adjacent angles:** Share a vertex and a side.
- **Linear pair:** Adjacent supplementary angles on a straight line (sum 180¬į).
- **Transversal:** A line that intersects two or more other lines.
- **Corresponding angles:** Same position at each intersection (congruent if lines are parallel).
- **Alternate interior / exterior:** Opposite sides of the transversal, between or outside the parallels (congruent if parallel).

### Important Formulas / Rules

| Relationship | Equation |
|--------------|----------|
| Complementary | `m‚ą†A + m‚ą†B = 90¬į` |
| Supplementary | `m‚ą†A + m‚ą†B = 180¬į` |
| Linear pair | `m‚ą†1 + m‚ą†2 = 180¬į` |
| Parallel + transversal | Corresponding, alternate interior, alternate exterior angles are **equal** |

### Example 1 ‚ÄĒ Complementary

**Problem:** One angle measures 38¬į. Find its complement.

**Solution:** `90¬į ‚ąí 38¬į = 52¬į`

**Answer:** **52¬į**

### Example 2 ‚ÄĒ Parallel lines

**Problem:** Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One angle is 115¬į. Find a corresponding angle and an alternate interior angle.

**Solution:** Corresponding angle = **115¬į**. Alternate interior to 115¬į on the other side of the transversal = **115¬į** (or use 180¬į ‚ąí 115¬į = 65¬į for the interior partner on the same side).

**Answer:** **115¬į** (corresponding); alternate interior pair includes **115¬į** and **65¬į**

**1. Complementary angles**

**Problem:** Angles measure `(2x + 10)¬į` and `(3x ‚ąí 5)¬į` and are complementary. Find `x` and each angle.

**Solution:**
1. `(2x + 10) + (3x ‚ąí 5) = 90` ‚Üí `5x + 5 = 90` ‚Üí `5x = 85` ‚Üí `x = 17`
2. Angles: `2(17) + 10 = 44¬į`, `3(17) ‚ąí 5 = 46¬į` (check: 44 + 46 = 90)

**Answer:** **x = 17**; **44¬į** and **46¬į**

---

**2. Supplementary angles**

**Problem:** One angle is twice another; together they are supplementary. Find both angles.

**Solution:**
1. Let smaller = `a`, larger = `2a` ‚Üí `a + 2a = 180` ‚Üí `3a = 180` ‚Üí `a = 60`
2. Angles: **60¬į** and **120¬į**

**Answer:** **60¬į** and **120¬į**

---

**3. Linear pair**

**Problem:** Two angles form a linear pair. One is `(4x + 12)¬į`, the other `(2x + 6)¬į`. Find `x` and each angle.

**Solution:** `(4x + 12) + (2x + 6) = 180` ‚Üí `6x + 18 = 180` ‚Üí `6x = 162` ‚Üí `x = 27`  
Angles: **120¬į** and **60¬į**

**Answer:** **x = 27**; **120¬į**, **60¬į**

---

**4. Parallel lines ‚ÄĒ find an angle**

**Problem:** Parallel lines cut by a transversal; one angle is **72¬į**. Find the measure of its alternate interior angle.

**Solution:** Alternate interior to 72¬į on parallel lines is **congruent** ‚Üí **72¬į**

**Answer:** **72¬į**

---

**5. Vertical angles**

**Problem:** Two lines intersect. One angle is **(5x ‚ąí 7)¬į**, its vertical angle is **(3x + 31)¬į`. Find `x` and the angle measure.

**Solution:** Vertical angles are equal: `5x ‚ąí 7 = 3x + 31` ‚Üí `2x = 38` ‚Üí `x = 19` ‚Üí angle **88¬į**

**Answer:** **x = 19**; **88¬į**

**6. Multi-step parallel lines**

**Problem:** `m‚ą†1 = 110¬į` (corresponding to an interior angle in a parallel-line diagram). Find `m‚ą†2` if ‚ą†2 is alternate interior to the 70¬į angle on the same transversal.

**Solution:** If one interior angle is 70¬į, its alternate interior partner is **70¬į**. Use 180¬į ‚ąí 110¬į = 70¬į for co-interior on one side.

**Answer:** **70¬į** (when ‚ą†2 is alternate interior to 70¬į)

### Common Mistakes
- Mixing **90¬į** (complementary) with **180¬į** (supplementary).
- Assuming any two angles on a diagram are supplementary without a straight line.
- For parallel lines, confusing **corresponding** with **alternate interior**.

### Mini Summary
- Complementary ‚Üí **90¬į**; supplementary / linear pair ‚Üí **180¬į**.
- Parallel lines + transversal ‚Üí use **congruent** angle pairs to find missing measures.
