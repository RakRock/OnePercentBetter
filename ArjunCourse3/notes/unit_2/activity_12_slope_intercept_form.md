## Quick Review Notes

### Main Idea
The form **`y = mx + b`** is the easiest way to graph a line and read its slope and starting value. **`m`** is the slope; **`b`** is the **y-intercept** (where the line hits the y-axis).

### Key Vocabulary
- **Slope-intercept form:** `y = mx + b`.
- **m:** Slope (steepness and direction).
- **b:** y-intercept (value of `y` when `x = 0`).
- **Linear equation:** Graph is a straight line.
- **Discrete data:** Separate points (often whole numbers); **continuous:** values along a line.

### Important Formulas / Rules

**Remember:**

`y = mx + b`

| Part | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| `m` | Slope — change in y per change in x |
| `b` | y-intercept — starting height on graph |

[DIAGRAM:y_mx_b]

**Graphing steps:**
1. Plot `(0, b)` on the y-axis.  
2. Use slope `m` (change in y / change in x) to find a second point.  
3. Draw the line through the points.

### Visual Explanation

Start at **b** on the y-axis, then use **slope** (vertical change / horizontal change) to find another point and draw the line.

### Example 1

**Problem:** For `y = −3x + 7`, what are the slope and y-intercept?

**Solution:**
- Compare to `y = mx + b`: `m = −3`, `b = 7`  
- Slope **−3** (change in y = −3 when change in x = 1); y-intercept **(0, 7)**  

**Answer:** **m = −3**, **b = 7**

### Example 2

**Problem:** Graph `y = (1/2)x − 2`.

[DIAGRAM:graph_example]

**Solution:**
1. Plot y-intercept: **(0, −2)**  
2. Slope `1/2`: from (0, −2), use **vertical change 1, horizontal change 2** → (2, −1)  
3. Draw line through points  

**Answer:** Line through (0, −2) with gentle positive slope.

**1. Check Your Understanding #9 — Slope from a table**

| x | 0 | 2 | 4 |
|---|---|---|---|
| y | 11 | 7.5 | 4 |

**Solution:**
1. Change in y: `4 − 11 = −7` (using x = 0 and x = 4)  
2. Change in x: `4 − 0 = 4`  
3. Slope = `−7/4 = −1.75`  

**Answer:** **slope = −7/4** (or **−1.75**)

**2. Items 10–13 — Ben’s Bells (bells distributed over years)**

**Problem:** 1,000 bells in 2003; **2,000 more bells each year** after 2003. Write equation `N` = bells after `t` years since 2003. Find slope and y-intercept.

**Solution:**
1. Table: `t = 0` → `N = 1000`; `t = 1` → `3000`; `t = 2` → `5000`  
2. Equation: **`N = 2000t + 1000`**  
3. Slope **2000** = bells added per year; y-intercept **1000** = bells in year 2003  

**Answer:** **`N = 2000t + 1000`**; slope **2000 bells/year**; intercept **1000 bells**

**3. Activity 12 Practice #1–2 — Leaky bottle `y = −3x + 24`**

**Problem:** `y` = mL of water after `x` seconds. What do slope and y-intercept mean?

**Solution:**
1. Slope **−3** mL per second (water **decreases** 3 mL each second)  
2. y-intercept **24** = **24 mL** in the bottle at start (`x = 0`)  

**Answer:** **slope −3 mL/s**; **y-intercept 24 mL** at start

**4. Activity 12 Practice #4 — Point on a line**

**Problem:** Line with slope **−2** through **(3, 5)** also passes through **(−2, p)**. Find `p`.

**Solution:**
1. Change in x: `−2 − 3 = −5`  
2. Change in y: `slope × change in x = −2 × (−5) = 10`  
3. `p = 5 + 10 = 15`  

**Answer:** **`p = 15`** (choice A)

**5. Lesson 12-1 Practice #15 — Graph and slope from table**

| y | 10 | 8.5 | 7 |

**Solution:**
1. Slope: `(7 − 10)/(6 − 0) = −3/6 = −0.5`  
2. y-intercept: **10**  
3. Equation: **`y = −0.5x + 10`**

**Problem:** **$15 per day** plus **$8 service charge**. Write total cost `y` for `x` days.

### Common Mistakes
- Swapping **m** and **b** (slope is the number with `x`).
- Plotting **b** on the x-axis instead of the y-axis.
- Putting **change in x** over **change in y** (should be change in y over change in x).
- Forgetting that **negative slope** goes downhill left to right.

### Mini Summary
- **`y = mx + b`** → slope `m`, y-intercept `b`.
- Start at **(0, b)**, then use slope to find another point.
- **Steeper** |m| → steeper line.
- Compare lines by comparing **m** (which is steeper? which direction?).
