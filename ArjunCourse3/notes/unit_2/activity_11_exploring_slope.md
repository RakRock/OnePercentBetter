# Activity 11: Exploring Slope

[KEY]
**slope = change in y / change in x** (vertical change ÷ horizontal change, or **Δy / Δx**).  
Slope is the **constant rate of change** of a line — the same ratio between any two points on the line.
[/KEY]

## Quick Review Notes

### Main Idea
**Slope** measures how steep a line is and how fast `y` changes compared to `x`. It is the **constant rate of change** of a line: the ratio of **vertical change** to **horizontal change** between any two points on the line.

### Key Vocabulary
- **Slope:** Steepness of a line; **change in y** per **change in x**.
- **Vertical change:** How far you move up or down (same as **change in y**, or **Δy**).
- **Horizontal change:** How far you move left or right (same as **change in x**, or **Δx**).
- **Rate of change:** How much one quantity changes when another changes by 1 (also called **average rate of change** for a line).
- **y-intercept:** Where the line crosses the y-axis, `(0, y)`.

### Important Formulas / Rules

**Remember (from the book):**

`slope = vertical change / horizontal change = change in y / change in x`

You may also see: **Δy / Δx** (Δ means “change in”).

From two points `(x₁, y₁)` and `(x₂, y₂)`:

`slope = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁)`

[DIAGRAM:change_in_y_x]

**Positive slope:** line goes up left to right.  
**Negative slope:** line goes down left to right.  
**Zero slope:** horizontal line.

### Visual Explanation

Between two points on a line, draw a right triangle: **vertical change** is the up/down leg, **horizontal change** is the left/right leg. The slope is that ratio.

### Example 1

**Problem:** A line passes through `(2, 3)` and `(6, 11)`. Find the slope.

**Solution:**
- Change in y: `11 − 3 = 8`  
- Change in x: `6 − 2 = 4`  
- Slope: `8 / 4 = 2`  

**Answer:** **Slope = 2** (`y` increases by 2 when `x` increases by 1).

### Example 2

**Problem:** A bike travels 30 miles in 2 hours. What is the slope of distance vs. time?

[DIAGRAM:rate_context]

**Solution:**
- Change in distance: 30 mi  
- Change in time: 2 hr  
- Slope = `30 / 2 = 15` miles per hour  

**Answer:** **15 mi/hr** — speed is the slope (rate of change).

**1. Check Your Understanding #16 — John’s longboard**

**Problem:** After 2 minutes he is 1,000 ft from home; after 5 minutes, 2,500 ft. Find his **average rate of change** (ft per minute).

**Solution:**

**2. Lesson 11-1 Practice #19–20 — Tran family road trip**

**Problem:** They drive **400 miles per day**. Write an equation for total miles `M` after `d` days. Find **slope** and **y-intercept** and what they mean.

**Solution:**
1. Table: day 1 → 400, day 2 → 800 → `M = 400d`  
2. Slope = `change in M / change in d = 400/1 = 400` (miles per day)  
3. y-intercept: when `d = 0`, `M = 0` → **(0, 0)**  
4. Slope = **400 mi/day**; intercept **0** = miles before the trip starts  

**Answer:** **`M = 400d`**; slope **400** (miles per day); y-intercept **0**

**Solution:**

### Common Mistakes
- Putting **change in x** on top instead of **change in y**.
- Subtracting coordinates in **different order** on top vs. bottom.
- **Change in x = 0** (vertical line) — slope is **undefined**.
- Confusing **slope** with **y-intercept**.

### Mini Summary
- Slope = **change in y / change in x** = constant rate of change.
- Use a **table** or two points to find Δy and Δx.
- **Positive** slope → increasing; **negative** → decreasing.
- In real life, slope often means **per unit** (miles per hour, dollars per day).
